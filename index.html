Physical
Disabilities

This type of disability affects the body's ability to move, coordinate, or perform physical tasks. Examples include mobility impairments, such as paralysis or amputation, and conditions like arthritis or cerebral palsy.

Sensory
Disabilities

This type of disability affects the senses, including vision, hearing, or touch. Examples include blindness, deafness, and conditions like color blindness or tinnitus.

Cognitive
Disabilities

This type of disability affects cognitive functions, such as memory, attention, and problem-solving. Examples include conditions like Alzheimer's disease, ADHD, and autism spectrum disorders.

Intellectual
Disabilities

This type of disability affects intellectual functioning and adaptive behavior, including communication, social skills, and independent living. Examples include conditions like Down syndrome and intellectual developmental disorder.

Mental Health
Disabilities

This type of disability affects an individual's emotional and psychological well-being. Examples include conditions like depression, anxiety, and bipolar disorder.
